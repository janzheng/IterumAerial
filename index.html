Creative Movements

in the air, on the ground

Shannon is a flowy, whimsical, and light-hearted aerialist and classically trained ballet dancer with more than a decade of aerial and dance experience.

She has choreographed and performed ambient and original pieces, and delights in costuming and performing character work, including faeries, princesses, mermaids, and pixies.

Learn more

Shannon Stanley started performing on stage at the tender age of 4. Focusing on classical ballet, she received training at Central Pennsylvania Youth Ballet, Virginia Ballet Theater, Governor’s School for the Arts and Miami City Ballet. At 14, she was accepted into the apprentice program with the company at Virginia Ballet Theater and shortly after joined as a company member. Simultaneously performing with Governor’s School for the Arts, over this period of time she performed in many ballets such as Paquita, Sleeping Beauty, The Nutcracker, and the lead role in Balanchine’s Valse Fantasie.

Performing is her most powerful form of expression and hopes to continue to travel the globe sharing her movement with all audiences.

Shannon has performed original pieces for many private and public events, like at The Alzheimer’s Association, Emory ALS Center, and Voila Foundation. In addition to private parties, birthdays and weddings, she has created and performed original pieces for The Atlanta Aerial Arts Festival and local showcases such as Open Grip and Sky Jam.

Shannon is available for travel.

Teaching & Workshops

Creating Moments

in the air, on the ground

Shannon’s teaching style values technique and movement quality, exploring each student’s individual flair, style, and quirks, to help them develop a unique and deeply personal style of movement.

Previously, She has instructed courses at The Atlanta Aerial Arts Festival and hopes to increase her travel for instructor opportunities. She specializes in Aerial Silk, Aerial Sling, and Aerial Conditioning.

Learn more

Shannon Stanley began her formal movement training in ballet at the age of 3. At the age of 14, she became an apprentice with Virginia Ballet Theater, starting her journey as a ballet instructor and performer.

She began teaching aerial fabric and aerial conditioning in 2014. She has completed teacher training courses under the direction of Amber Monson as well as the Born to Fly teacher training under the direction of Rain Anya of The Paper Doll Militia.

She currently holds a busy schedule a both The Space and at Sky Gym. Take a look at her schedule.
